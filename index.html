Rows — Severity (harm if the diagnosis is missed/delayed)  ·  Columns — Difficulty (how hard the diagnosis is to make). Difficulty is protective: score = severity × ease, ease = 6 − difficulty. Missing an obvious, lethal diagnosis is the worst quality failure.

Rows — Potential harm (worst plausible harm the error could cause — from drug toxicity or under-treatment)  ·  Columns — Deviation (how far the therapy departs from correct — every column is a real error). Score = potential harm × deviation. Optimal care isn't on the grid. Even the leftmost column is a real (if trivial) departure from the single preferred option — which is why a life-threatening illness with only a negligible deviation still scores 5 (Minor), not zero. Worst failure = a contraindicated choice with fatal potential.
